What to Do in the City

Boston is a beautiful city with lots to do. There are many different neighborhoods to explore, each with something special. On this page, you'll find some of my favorite neighborhoods to explore in the city.

The North End

The North End is Bostonâ€™s "Little Italy." With a waterfront view, it has some of the oldest buildings in the city, and is filled with Italian restaurants.

Fenway-Kenmore

There is never a dull moment in this neighborhood. Here you can find Fenway Park (the oldest baseball stadium in the country) as well as famous museums like the Museum of Fine Arts.

Back Bay

In this neighborhood, go shopping and eat at a restaurant along Newbury Street's beautiful brick apartments. In Copley Square, visitors can find historic landmarks like Trinity Church or the Boston Public Library.

Best Spots to Visit in Back Bay

  • Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum
  • Boston Common (the oldest public park in US history!)
  • Newbury Street
  • Boston Public Library
  • Gibson House Museum
